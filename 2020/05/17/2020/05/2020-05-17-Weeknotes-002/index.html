Weeknotes-002

James
2020-05-17

What a month that week was! I’ve been in really busy in final delivery for my current project at work so haven’t had a huge amount of time to work on this blog. I had planned to start making another post looking at composition of shots and what goes in to them, that’ll come soon. With the sad, inevitable slow down in post production that will be coming our way due to most worldwide filming pausing for C-19 I’ll hopefully get a bit more time over the summer to work on other hobbies.

(Un)real Engine

Well at least we know what the next Tomb Raider is going to look like. Whilst of course so much of what is seen in this demo is down to the wizard and not the wand, the technology on display here leaves me in awe of what is now possible (as long as you have a few TB spare on your hard drive). Realtime lighting has always been a far off dream in VFX (I’m still yet to see katana able to put a checkerboard on a plane and do real time lighting). Of course, there are tricks in play here that perhaps wouldn’t really stand up to photoreal scrutiny, but I would still take this over a 4 hour+ render any day when it can be tweaked further in comp with the right passes. Lifting the polygon limit, as they claim here, I think still needs some looking at if it’s really true - this has been one of the problems with using game engines in VFX in the past.

If this is really a true demo using realistic (as in reasonably sized) assets and textures (and I think it’s a big if at the moment) then it’s a genuine game changer. I imagine this 5 minute demo might be taking 500GB+, but still it’s beautiful… I’ll hopefully give this a go one of these days and see what it can do.

Anyone who knows my gaming habits knows Tomb Raider is my absolute favourite all time franchise - pleeeease let the next TR game look even half as good as this!

Around the Web

  • We should be very wary of the R value

The R0 rate is apparently climbing back to near 1 in many places. This is an easy headline for media to report how everything is burning down, but closer analysis of this means R0 climbing a bit is actually a good thing and showing that the lockdown is working. This is due to an interesting quirk of statistics called Simpson’s Paradox.
